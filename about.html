Forty‑two years of showing up on time.

Meridian Post started as a two‑van letter route and grew into a 480‑city network — without losing the habits that made it trustworthy in the first place.

Our beginning

Built on one honest route.

In 1984, two founders and a single delivery van covered eleven streets in Ashford. The promise was simple: if we said it would arrive Tuesday, it arrived Tuesday. That promise is still the whole business plan.

Today the same principle runs twenty‑one regional hubs — just at a scale the founders never quite pictured.

What guides us

Three habits we don't compromise on.

01

Show up when we say

Delivery windows are commitments, not estimates. Our routing exists to protect that.

02

Make it traceable

Every parcel is scannable at every handoff — no unexplained gaps in the timeline.

03

Handle it like it's yours

Carriers are trained to treat every package the way they'd want their own handled.
